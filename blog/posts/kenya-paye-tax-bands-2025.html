Introduction

Pay As You Earn (PAYE) is Kenya's primary system for collecting income tax from employed individuals. Under this system, employers are required to deduct tax from their employees' employment income and remit the tax to the Kenya Revenue Authority (KRA). This comprehensive guide covers the 2025 PAYE tax bands, recent changes, and everything you need to know about income tax calculations in Kenya.

2025 PAYE Tax Bands and Rates

The current Individual Income Tax Rates range from 10% to 35% as per Finance Act 2023, effective from 1st July 2023. Here are the official tax bands for 2025:

Monthly Tax Bands

Income Range (KES) Tax Rate
First 24,000 10%
Next 8,333 (24,001 - 32,333) 25%
Next 467,667 (32,334 - 500,000) 30%
Next 300,000 (500,001 - 800,000) 32.5%

Personal Relief and Tax Reliefs

Personal Relief

All resident individuals are entitled to a personal relief of KES 2,400 per month or KES 28,800 per year. This relief is automatically applied to reduce your final tax liability.

Important Changes to Tax Reliefs

Effective December 1, 2024, reliefs now ONLY include the Monthly Personal Relief of KES 2,400. The previously applicable SHIF Relief of 15% and the 15% tax relief for the affordable housing levy were both repealed by the Kenya Tax Laws (Amendment) Act, 2024.

Insurance Relief

Insurance relief is granted to employees who have paid insurance premiums for life, health, or education policies for themselves, their spouse, or child. Relief is given at 15% of premiums paid up to a maximum of KES 60,000 per annum.

Allowable Deductions for 2025

The following amounts can be deducted from your gross salary before calculating PAYE:

1. Social Health Insurance Fund (SHIF)

  • Rate: 2.75% of monthly gross pay
  • Minimum Contribution: KES 300 per month
  • Status: SHIF contributions became allowable deductions as of December 2024

2. National Social Security Fund (NSSF) - New 2025 Rates

Effective February 1, 2025, the NSSF rates increased significantly with the Lower Earnings Limit (LEL) increasing from KES 7,000 to KES 8,000 and the Upper Earnings Limit (UEL) increasing from KES 36,000 to KES 72,000.

New NSSF Structure (February 2025):
  • Tier I: 6% of the first KES 8,000 (KES 480 each from employer and employee)
  • Tier II: 6% of earnings between KES 8,001 and KES 72,000 (up to KES 3,840 each)
  • Maximum Monthly Contribution: KES 4,320 per party

3. Affordable Housing Levy (AHL)

  • Employee Contribution: 1.5% of gross monthly salary
  • Employer Contribution: 1.5% of employee's monthly gross salary
  • Status: The AHL became an allowable deduction as of December 2024
  • No Cap: Unlike other contributions, the housing levy has no upper limit

4. Other Allowable Deductions

Additional deductions include:

  • Pension Contributions: Up to KES 30,000 per month (KES 360,000 per year)
  • Mortgage Interest: Up to KES 30,000 per month for owner-occupied residential property
  • Post-Retirement Medical Fund: Up to KES 15,000 per month

Taxable Employment Income

Taxable employment income includes all cash payments however described and the value of non-cash benefits exceeding KES 5,000 per month. This encompasses:

  • Wages, salary, sick pay, leave pay
  • Fees, commissions, bonuses
  • Service gratuity allowances
  • Director's fees, overtime, pension
  • Entertainment and other employment-related payments

Non-Taxable Benefits

The following benefits are not subject to PAYE:

  • Meals provided by employer up to KES 5,000 per month
  • Night-out allowance of KES 2,000 per day
  • Medical cover provided by employer
  • Travel expenses for non-Kenyan citizens serving in Kenya

PAYE Calculation Example

Let's calculate PAYE for someone earning KES 100,000 per month:

Example: Monthly Salary KES 100,000

Gross Salary: KES 100,000

Deductions:

  • NSSF: KES 4,320 (capped amount)
  • SHIF: KES 2,750 (2.75% of 100,000)
  • Housing Levy: KES 1,500 (1.5% of 100,000)

Taxable Income: KES 100,000 - KES 8,570 = KES 91,430

PAYE Calculation:

  • First KES 24,000 @ 10% = KES 2,400
  • Next KES 8,333 @ 25% = KES 2,083
  • Remaining KES 59,097 @ 30% = KES 17,729
  • Total PAYE: KES 22,212
  • Less Personal Relief: KES 2,400
  • Net PAYE: KES 19,812

Compliance and Due Dates

For Employers

Employers must deduct PAYE from employees' salaries and remit the amounts to KRA on or before the 9th day of the following month. A PAYE return must also be filed through iTax on or before the 9th day of the following month.

Penalties for Non-Compliance

Late filing attracts the higher of 25% of the tax due or KES 10,000. Late payment incurs a penalty of 5% of the tax due plus 1% interest per month on unpaid tax.

Housing Levy Penalties

Failure to remit the Affordable Housing Levy by the due date attracts a penalty of 3% per month on the outstanding amount.

Key Changes in 2025

1. Enhanced NSSF Contributions

The NSSF Act 2013 entered its third implementation phase in February 2025, with tier I contributions increasing from KES 420 to KES 480 and tier II contributions increasing from KES 1,740 to KES 3,840. This represents the most significant change to retirement savings in Kenya since the implementation of the NSSF Act.

The government aims to amass KES 1 trillion through these increased contributions by 2027, making NSSF one of the largest pension funds in Africa. This gradual implementation was designed to ease the transition for both employers and employees while ensuring adequate retirement savings for Kenyan workers.

2. Allowable Deductions for SHIF and Housing Levy

The shift to treat AHL and SHIF as allowable deductions is beneficial to employees, as it lowers the taxable income, reducing the overall tax liability. This change partially offsets the increased financial burden that these levies initially created when they were introduced.

3. Removal of Certain Tax Reliefs

The 15% reliefs for SHIF contributions and housing levy payments were repealed in December 2024, simplifying the tax calculation process. This streamlining measure reduces administrative complexity while ensuring that the tax system remains progressive and equitable.

Digital Tax Compliance: The iTax System

Overview of iTax Platform

Kenya Revenue Authority's iTax system is the primary platform for all tax compliance activities. The system has been continuously updated to accommodate new tax requirements and streamline the filing process.

PAYE Filing Through iTax

Employers must file monthly PAYE returns through iTax by following these steps:

  1. Login: Access iTax using your KRA PIN and password
  2. Navigate: Click on "Returns" tab and select "File Return"
  3. Download: Download the Excel return form (P10)
  4. Complete: Fill in employee details and tax calculations
  5. Validate: Click "VALIDATE" at the end of Sheet N_Tax Due
  6. Upload: Upload the zipped return file
  7. Submit: Agree to terms and conditions and submit

Payment Methods

Multiple payment options are available:

  • Bank Payment: Using the generated payment slip
  • M-PESA: Paybill 222222 with Payment Registration Number as account
  • Debit/Credit Card: Through the iTax portal
  • RTGS: For large payments

Impact Analysis: Take-Home Pay Changes in 2025

Comparative Analysis: 2024 vs 2025

The combination of increased NSSF contributions and the introduction of SHIF and housing levy significantly impacts employees' disposable income. While the treatment of AHL and SHIF as allowable deductions provides some relief, the increased NSSF contributions will further decrease employees' net pay, limiting their disposable income.

Sample Impact Analysis (KES 100,000 Gross Salary)

2024 Deductions:

  • NSSF (Old Rate): KES 2,160
  • NHIF: KES 1,700
  • Housing Levy: KES 1,500
  • Total: KES 5,360

2025 Deductions:

  • NSSF (New Rate): KES 4,320
  • SHIF: KES 2,750
  • Housing Levy: KES 1,500
  • Total: KES 8,570

Net Impact: Additional KES 3,210 in deductions, but these are now allowable deductions reducing taxable income by the same amount, resulting in tax savings of approximately KES 963 (30% tax bracket), for a net increase in deductions of KES 2,247.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

General PAYE Questions

Q: What happens if my employer makes an error in PAYE deduction?

A: Employees should immediately notify their employer of any errors. Employers can correct overpayments in subsequent months or through amended returns. For underpayments, employees may be liable for additional tax.

Q: Can I claim additional deductions beyond the standard allowances?

A: Yes, you can claim mortgage interest (up to KES 30,000/month), pension contributions (up to KES 30,000/month), insurance premiums, and post-retirement medical fund contributions (up to KES 15,000/month).

Q: How does PAYE apply to bonus payments?

A: Bonuses are treated as regular employment income and subject to PAYE at applicable rates. Large bonuses may push you into higher tax brackets for the month received.

NSSF-Related Questions

Q: What happens to my old NSSF contributions?

A: All previous contributions remain in your account and continue to earn returns. The new rates only apply to future contributions.

Q: Can I make voluntary NSSF contributions above the statutory amount?

A: Yes, voluntary contributions are allowed and may qualify for tax relief up to the KES 30,000 monthly limit for pension contributions.

SHIF and Housing Levy Questions

Q: What services does SHIF cover compared to the old NHIF?

A: SHIF provides more comprehensive coverage including outpatient services, chronic disease management, and emergency care with better provider networks.

Q: Can I opt out of the housing levy if I already own a home?

A: No, the housing levy is mandatory for all employees regardless of homeownership status. However, future purchases under government housing schemes may qualify for tax relief.

Q: Are expatriate employees subject to housing levy and SHIF?

A: Yes, all employees working in Kenya are subject to these deductions regardless of citizenship, unless specifically exempted under applicable tax treaties.

Conclusion

Kenya's 2025 PAYE system represents a significant evolution in the country's approach to income taxation and social security. The system successfully balances multiple objectives: generating revenue for government operations, funding critical social programs, and maintaining economic competitiveness in the region.

The current framework reflects years of careful policy development, incorporating lessons learned from previous implementations and responding to changing economic realities. The progressive tax structure ensures that higher earners contribute proportionally more while providing meaningful relief for lower-income workers through personal allowances and allowable deductions.

Key Policy Achievements

The 2025 system achieves several important policy objectives:

Social Security Enhancement: The increased NSSF contributions will substantially improve retirement security for Kenyan workers, addressing long-standing concerns about adequate pension provision.

Universal Health Coverage: SHIF implementation marks a historic step toward ensuring all Kenyans have access to quality healthcare services, reducing financial barriers to medical treatment.

Housing Development: The housing levy provides dedicated funding for affordable housing initiatives, addressing one of Kenya's most pressing social challenges.

Tax System Modernization: The integration of digital compliance tools and streamlined processes reduces administrative burden while improving collection efficiency.

Key Takeaways for Employees

  • Tax Rates: PAYE rates remain unchanged since July 2023, ranging from 10% to 35% in a progressive structure
  • Personal Relief: All residents receive KES 2,400 monthly personal relief
  • Increased Deductions: NSSF contributions increased significantly in February 2025, but SHIF and Housing Levy are now allowable deductions
  • Net Impact: While total deductions increased, the tax relief from allowable deductions partially offsets the higher burden
  • Planning Opportunity: Optimize additional allowable deductions like pension contributions and mortgage interest

Key Takeaways for Employers

  • Compliance Deadlines: All filings and payments must be completed by the 9th of the following month
  • System Updates: Payroll systems must be updated to reflect current rates and new deduction categories
  • Documentation: Maintain comprehensive records of all calculations and supporting documentation
  • Training: Ensure payroll staff understand current requirements and calculation methods
  • Professional Support: Consider engaging tax professionals for complex situations or compliance assurance

Understanding these changes is crucial for effective financial planning. Employees should review their payslips carefully to ensure correct deductions, while employers must update their payroll systems to comply with the new requirements and avoid penalties. Professional advice may be valuable for complex situations or significant financial decisions.

The PAYE system will continue to evolve as Kenya's economy grows and social needs change. Staying informed about these developments and planning accordingly will help individuals and businesses navigate the system successfully while contributing to Kenya's broader development objectives.
