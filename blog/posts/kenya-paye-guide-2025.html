Understanding Kenya's PAYE Tax in 2025

A complete guide to Kenya's Pay As You Earn (PAYE) tax system, rates, and how to calculate your take-home pay.

What is PAYE?

Pay As You Earn (PAYE) is the tax deducted from your salary every month in Kenya. It's calculated based on your gross income and follows specific tax bands set by the Kenya Revenue Authority (KRA).

üí° Key Point: PAYE is deducted automatically by your employer, so you don't need to pay it separately.

2025 PAYE Tax Rates

Kenya uses a progressive tax system with these rates:

  • First KSh 24,000: 10%
  • Next KSh 8,333: 25%
  • Above KSh 32,333: 30%

üí∞ Example: KSh 80,000 Monthly Salary

First KSh 24,000: KSh 2,400 (10%)

Next KSh 8,333: KSh 2,083 (25%)

Remaining KSh 47,667: KSh 14,300 (30%)

Total PAYE: KSh 18,783

Other Deductions to Consider

Besides PAYE, your salary may also include these deductions:

NSSF (National Social Security Fund)

NSSF contributions are calculated at 6% of your pensionable pay, with both you and your employer contributing equally (6% each, totaling 12%).

NHIF (National Hospital Insurance Fund)

NHIF rates are based on your gross salary and range from KSh 150 to KSh 1,700 per month depending on your income bracket.

üéØ Pro Tip: These deductions are made before calculating PAYE, which can reduce your taxable income!

How to Calculate Your Net Pay

To calculate your take-home pay:

  1. Start with your gross salary
  2. Subtract NSSF contribution
  3. Subtract other allowable deductions
  4. Calculate PAYE on the remaining amount
  5. Subtract PAYE and NHIF to get your net pay

üí∞ Complete Example: KSh 100,000 Gross Salary

Gross Salary: KSh 100,000

Less NSSF (6%): KSh 6,000

Taxable Income: KSh 94,000

PAYE Tax: KSh 24,500

Less NHIF: KSh 1,700

Net Pay: KSh 67,800

Tax Relief and Personal Relief

Every Kenyan taxpayer is entitled to a personal relief of KSh 2,400 per month (KSh 28,800 annually). This amount is deducted from your calculated PAYE tax.

Tips for Managing Your PAYE

Understanding your PAYE helps you better plan your finances:

  • Review your payslip monthly to understand deductions
  • Keep records for tax compliance
  • Consider voluntary contributions to pension schemes for tax benefits
  • Use salary calculators to plan career moves
