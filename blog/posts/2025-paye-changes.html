The Kenya Revenue Authority (KRA) has announced significant changes to the PAYE tax structure for 2025. These changes affect millions of Kenyan employees and will impact take-home salaries across all income levels. Here's everything you need to know about the new tax brackets, rates, and how they affect your paycheck.

Key Changes at a Glance

üéØ Quick Summary of 2025 Changes:

  • New tax brackets with adjusted income thresholds
  • Introduction of 32.5% tax bracket for high earners
  • Personal relief remains at KES 2,400 per month
  • SHIF replaces NHIF with 2.75% contribution rate
  • Housing Levy continues at 1.5% of gross salary

New PAYE Tax Brackets for 2025

The 2025 tax year introduces a more progressive tax structure with five distinct brackets:

Monthly Income (KES) Tax Rate What's New
0 - 24,000 10% No change
24,001 - 32,333 25% No change
32,334 - 500,000 30% Extended bracket
500,001 - 800,000 32.5% New bracket
Above 800,000 35% Top rate

How the Changes Affect Different Income Levels

Low-Income Earners (Below KES 50,000)

Employees earning below KES 50,000 per month will see minimal changes to their tax obligations. The first two tax brackets remain unchanged, providing stability for lower-income workers.

Middle-Income Earners (KES 50,000 - 200,000)

This group experiences the most significant impact. While the 30% rate remains, the extended bracket means more of their income is taxed at this rate rather than jumping to higher brackets.

High-Income Earners (Above KES 500,000)

The introduction of the 32.5% bracket creates a more gradual progression to the top 35% rate, potentially resulting in tax savings for those earning between KES 500,000 and KES 800,000.

Other Important Deductions

Social Health Insurance Fund (SHIF)

The transition from NHIF to SHIF brings a new contribution structure:

  • Rate: 2.75% of gross salary
  • Minimum contribution: KES 300
  • No maximum cap

Housing Levy

The Housing Levy continues at 1.5% of gross salary with both employer and employee contributing equally. This fund supports the government's affordable housing agenda.

NSSF Contributions

NSSF rates remain unchanged with Tier I and Tier II contributions based on pensionable earnings.

Tax Relief Opportunities

Despite the changes, several tax relief options remain available:

üí° Available Tax Reliefs:

  • Pension contributions: Up to KES 30,000 monthly
  • Insurance relief: 15% of premiums, max KES 5,000 monthly
  • Mortgage interest: Up to KES 30,000 monthly for owner-occupied property
  • Post-retirement medical fund: Up to KES 15,000 monthly

Planning for the Changes

To optimize your tax position under the new structure:

  1. Review your deductions: Ensure you're maximizing available reliefs
  2. Consider pension contributions: Additional voluntary contributions can reduce taxable income
  3. Update your budget: Account for changes in net income
  4. Consult a tax professional: For complex situations or high incomes

Conclusion

The 2025 PAYE changes represent a significant shift in Kenya's tax landscape. While some employees may see increased deductions, the new structure aims to create a more equitable distribution of the tax burden. Understanding these changes is crucial for financial planning and ensuring you're not paying more tax than necessary.
