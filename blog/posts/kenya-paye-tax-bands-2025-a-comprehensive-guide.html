PAYE tax rates in Kenya range from 10% to 35% based on income brackets. A monthly personal relief of KSh 2,400 (KSh 28,800 annually) applies to all taxpayers. NSSF contributions (Tier I & II) and SHIF at 2.75% are mandatory statutory deductions. The Affordable Housing Levy (1.5% from both employer and employee) is fully tax-deductible. Finance Bill 2025 mandates automatic tax relief application in payroll from July 2025. Non-cash benefits like cars and housing may be taxable if they exceed set limits.

PAYE is a progressive tax where employees' income is taxed at different rates based on earnings. The Finance Act 2023 set the current tax bands, which remain through 2025. Understanding these bands helps ensure accurate tax compliance.

Understanding PAYE (Pay As You Earn)

Definition and Purpose PAYE is a method where employers deduct income tax from salaries and remit it to the KRA. It ensures regular tax collection and simplifies the process for individuals.

Who Is Subject to PAYE? Anyone earning above KSh 24,000 per month, including residents and non-residents with income in Kenya. Employers must register with KRA and manage PAYE compliance.
